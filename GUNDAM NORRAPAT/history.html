Heritage of Jazz — A Timeline

A timeline of major jazz eras.

Key Eras & Moments

  1. 1900s–1920s

    Early Jazz — New Orleans

    Jazz began in New Orleans as a fusion of African rhythms, brass-band tradition, blues, and ragtime. It introduced collective improvisation and community music-making.

    Notable figures

    Buddy Bolden, Jelly Roll Morton, Louis Armstrong (emerging late 1910s—1920s).

  2. 1930s–1940s

    Swing & Big Band

    Big bands and swing rhythms dominated dance halls. Arrangements became more elaborate and jazz reached mainstream audiences via radio and records.

    Notable figures

    Duke Ellington, Count Basie, Benny Goodman, Ella Fitzgerald.

  3. 1940s–1950s

    Bebop & Modern Jazz

    Bebop focused on small combos, virtuosic solos, and harmonic complexity — a turning point toward listening and artistry rather than dancing.

    Notable figures

    Charlie Parker, Dizzy Gillespie, Thelonious Monk.

  4. 1950s–1960s

    Cool, Modal, & Hard Bop

    Multiple directions: cool jazz with relaxed tones, modal jazz emphasizing scales, and hard bop blending gospel and blues influences.

    Notable figures

    Miles Davis, John Coltrane, Chet Baker.

  5. 1970s–Today

    Fusion, Global Jazz & Contemporary

    Electric instruments, rock and world-music elements, and technological advances expanded jazz's vocabulary. Contemporary artists continue to blend genres.

    Notable figures

    Miles Davis (electric era), Herbie Hancock, modern global artists and cross-genre collaborators.
