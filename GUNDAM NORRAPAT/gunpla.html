Materials & Setup

Essentials: dual-action airbrush, compressor, quality primer, acrylic/lacquer paints, sanding set (400–2000 grit), masking tape, decal solutions, and a good lamp. Safety: ventilate, use respirators for spray work, and keep thinners away from flames.

Favorite Finishes

  • Matte: realistic military or worn look
  • Satin: balanced sheen for display
  • Gloss + Candy: metallic base + tinted clear for depth
  • Weathered: washes, pigments, and chipping
Airbrush tips ▾

Keep consistent distance (~10–20 cm), keep the gun moving, and use multiple thin passes. Clean thoroughly between colors and always test spray on scrap.

Masking tips ▾

Use high-quality low-tack tape, press edges firmly, and leave the tape on until the paint is fully cured to avoid lifting.

Weathering recipes ▾

Simple wash: dark enamel thinned heavily; apply to recesses and wick away excess. Pigments: mix with water/alcohol binder; dab and fix with matte sealer. Chipping: sponge or hairspray base-coat method for realistic edges.

Detailed Workflow

Step-by-step

  1. Prep — remove sprues carefully, deburr and test-fit parts.
  2. Prime — use light coats, inspect for seams and imperfections.
  3. Sanding — work up through grits; wet-sanding for a glass finish.
  4. Base coats — thin paint 1.5–2.5x depending on brand; multiple passes.
  5. Mask & detail — crisp color separations and panel accents.
  6. Decals — gloss base -> set -> sol -> seal -> final topcoat.
  7. Photo & display — lighting, backdrop, and composition make the finish sing.

Photography Tips

  • Use soft directional light (softbox) to show finish without harsh reflections.
  • Neutral background (gray/black/white) keeps focus on the model.
  • Use tripod and small aperture (f/8–f/11) for sharp detail.

Quick checklist

Common mistakes & fixes

  • Thick coats -> runs. Fix: sand & re-prime, thin paint more.
  • Silvering under decals -> use gloss base before decals.
  • Uneven masking -> use better tape and burnish edges.
